About Us

We are an organization run by high school students in the DMV. Our mission is simple: share the coding knowledge we know with fellow students of all ages so they can also engage in the world of programming. Here's a little more information about us.

Our mission

KidCoders 365 is an organization that offers free, interactive programming classes for elementary, middle, and high school students, run by high school students in Maryland. Today’s kids often feel that there are limited opportunities to learn full programming languages. Our organization seeks to provide youth with free, unrestricted opportunities to explore computer programming, a vital skill in the modern world, as well as essential skills related to the subject.

Partnerships

The Rockville Memorial Library is a branch of Montgomery County Public Libraries, a government organization whose mission is to "[offer] free and equal access to services and resources that connect the people of Montgomery County to ideas and information which sustain and enrich their lives."

Discover STEAM Inc. is a "501(c)(3) innovative educational outreach company focused on Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics." Its mission is to "provide engaging STEAM activities to all youth with a focus on including under-served, under-represented and/or disadvantaged students to promote college and career interests in STEAM."

Arush Arora

President | Founder | Instructor

Hey I'm Arush, a senior at Richard Montgomery High School in the IB Magnet Program. I am an avid programmer and love to code in Python and Java. I learned Python independently in sixth grade and Java through John Hopkins University's Center for Talented Youth. I enjoy working on personal programming projects, in addition to course-related work, and have developed games such as battleship and pong from scratch. Currently, I am working on adding an AI player to the games I have built using Machine Learning modules like TensorFlow and PyTorch.

From 2020–2022, I taught Python through Rockville Memorial Library's (MCPL) TeenHub program andthoroughly enjoyed the experience of teaching kids. I decided to offer free Python classes over thesummer to share my passion for programming with more kids. I am excited to be coordinating and teachingprogramming classes for elementary, middle, and high school students.
